Quality policy

We will not supply a device that fails its own specification.

This is the single line every AVIORA team member and supply-chain partner signs onto. Deadlines are managed through capacity planning, not through relaxed acceptance criteria. Devices that don't pass don't ship.

"Quality is built into the device at the point of manufacture, verified at import, and confirmed on every lot — not assumed from a supplier's certificate alone."

— AVIORA MedTech Quality Policy Statement

Quality process

Ten checkpoints, every shipment.

01

Supplier Qualification

Manufacturing partners are audited and approved against documented criteria — ISO 13485, CE certification and CDSCO import eligibility — before any purchase order is raised.

02

Manufacturing Standards Review

Partner quality management systems, SOPs and production controls are reviewed against ISO 13485:2016 and relevant device standards before supply agreements are signed.

03

Incoming Inspection

Every incoming batch is sampled and checked against product specifications — including packaging integrity, label compliance and lot documentation — before release to stock.

04

Product Traceability

Every unit carries a lot number linking it to the manufacturing batch, sterilisation cycle and import record. Lot data is retained for the full shelf-life period plus five years.

05

Sterility Assurance

All devices are gamma or EO sterilised by the manufacturing partner, with cycle validation records and biological indicator test results available on request for each lot.

06

Regulatory Compliance

Import documentation, CDSCO registration and device-category compliance are maintained and reviewed at every re-import. Non-compliant lots are quarantined, not released.

07

CE Documentation

CE technical files and Declaration of Conformity documents for applicable product lines are maintained in our quality records and available for hospital tender submissions.

08

FDA Documentation

For product lines where FDA 510(k) clearance applies, clearance letters and K-numbers are documented. Status is confirmed per product on request.

09

Complaint Handling & CAPA

Every field complaint is logged, investigated and, where required, run through a formal CAPA process. Root-cause findings feed back to manufacturing partners for corrective action.

10

Continuous Improvement

Annual partner audits, internal process reviews and post-market surveillance data drive ongoing improvement across sourcing, logistics and customer support.

Supplier qualification

How we qualify every manufacturing partner.

No supplier ships a device under the AVIORA name until they have passed every stage of this process. Approved status is reviewed annually and can be withdrawn if standards slip.

01

Documentary Review

ISO 13485 certificate, CE technical files, ANVISA / CDSCO registration, and production SOPs are reviewed and verified against the applicable regulatory requirements before any further engagement.

02

Technical Evaluation

Sample devices are evaluated against published specifications — dimensional tolerances, staple formation heights, closure force, and sterility assurance levels — using documented acceptance criteria.

03

Quality System Audit

A structured audit of the manufacturing partner's quality management system — covering incoming inspection, in-process controls, final release and complaint handling — is completed before approval is granted.

04

Supply Agreement & Approval

A formal supply agreement specifying quality obligations, lot documentation requirements, change-control notification and CAPA response timelines is signed before the first commercial order is placed.

05

Annual Re-qualification

Every approved partner is re-audited on an annual cycle. Certificate renewals, field complaint trends and any manufacturing changes are reviewed. Approved status is not permanent — it is maintained by performance.

06

Approved Supplier Register

All approved manufacturing partners are maintained on a controlled Approved Supplier Register. Procurement is permitted only from listed suppliers. Additions or removals require a documented change-control record.

Risk management & post-market surveillance

Monitoring device safety from first shipment through end of shelf life.

Our post-market activities are designed to identify safety signals early — before they reach the operating theatre a second time.

ISO 14971 Risk Management

Manufacturing partners are required to maintain a risk management file aligned to ISO 14971 for each device in our range, with risk acceptability reviewed at each design change.

Complaint Logging & Trending

All field complaints are logged within 24 hours, coded by type and device, and reviewed monthly for trends. Any signal above threshold triggers a formal investigation regardless of severity.

Vigilance & Adverse Event Reporting

Serious adverse events are reported to CDSCO in accordance with the Medical Devices Rules 2017. Manufacturing partners are notified within 48 hours and required to initiate root-cause analysis.

Field Safety Corrective Actions

Where a FSCA or recall is required, AVIORA MedTech coordinates device retrieval through our distributor and hospital network, with full traceability back to affected lot numbers.
