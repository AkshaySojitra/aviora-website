Quality system

Six checkpoints. Zero shortcuts.

Every Aviora device is manufactured under an ISO 13485:2016 quality management system, with documented control from supplier qualification through final release.

Quality policy

We will not ship a device that fails its own specification — even under delivery pressure.

This is the single line every operator, supervisor and quality reviewer at Aviora signs onto. Deadlines are managed through capacity planning, not through relaxed acceptance criteria.

Process

From raw material to release note.

01

Supplier Qualification

Raw material and component suppliers are audited and approved against documented criteria before any purchase order is raised. Approved supplier status is reviewed annually.

02

Incoming Inspection

Every incoming batch of titanium wire, polymer resin or moulded component is sampled and inspected against acceptance specifications before release to production.

03

In-Process Quality Assurance

Staple formation, jaw closure force and dimensional checks are performed at defined intervals during production runs, not only at the end of the line.

04

Regulatory Compliance

Products are manufactured under CDSCO registration and designed to meet applicable IEC and ISO device standards for their category.

05

Final Release & Sterility Confirmation

Sterilisation cycle records and final inspection results are reviewed and signed off before a lot is released to the warehouse for dispatch.

06

Continuous Improvement

Field feedback, complaint data and internal audit findings feed a structured CAPA process that closes the loop back into design and production.
